Who are Trading Platforms and Brokers International Association?

Trading Platforms and Brokers International Association is an independent organization doing business in settlement of disputes that arise between traders on the one hand and trading platforms and brokers on the other.

What is your main goal?

TPBIA acts as a neutral facilitator between traders and brokers for an objective consideration of claims and resolving disputes.

What are your next steps after receiving a claim?

At first, we check whether a broker has already reviewed this claim. Then, we analyze the claim and contact the broker for details. We can also ask for additional information from a Claimant or broker. Next, we make a decision based on all the provided information. The Claimant receives the reply within 10 workdays after claim submission.

Do you share your decisions in public?

The decisions are sent directly via email to a Claimant and a broker, and never published anywhere in public.

What is the TPBIA fund?

TThe TPBIA Fund serves as a Claimant's insurance. All funds are kept on a separate bank account and used to meet the claims. The TPBIA Fund is financed from a part of membership fees.

Each trader who is a client of a broker or trading platform that are the members of the Association can count on a claim consideration by TBPIA.

Am I obliged to accept the decisions of TPBIA?

A Claimant is obliged to accept the decisions of TPBIA regarding disputes and comply with it. If a Claimant does not agree with the decision of the Association, then it has a right to seek for another decision by any other available means.

Which information should be included in a claim?

All claims and complaints should include the following:

  • - First, last and patronymic names (if available).
  • - Email address of a Claimant.
  • - A copy of the client's ID.
  • - Certain date and time of the issue.
  • - Brief description of an issue.
  • - A detailed description of the claim.
  • - Claimed amount with calculations proving its reasonableness (if a claim concerns funds).
  • - Explanation of circumstances that are the basis of the claim and proof of these circumstances.
  • - A list of documents and other evidence that are attached to a claim. All these documents must be certified by a Claimant.
  • - Any additional information that may help to resolve a dispute.

Additionally, claims SHOULD NOT contain:

  • - any emotional evaluation;
  • - offensive statements addressed to a broker or the Association;
  • - rude expressions.

The claims that not meet these criteria will not be reviewed.
